The Last Acorn

2D Puzzle Platformer • Unity • CS 247G Project

Play Demo on itch.io

About The Project

The Last Acorn is a 2D puzzle platformer created for Stanford's CS 247G (Designed for Play) class. Players control a mother squirrel on a quest to collect acorns while navigating through forests and dangerous caves. The game features custom-built movement mechanics including climbing, jumping, and gliding that work seamlessly together to create engaging platforming challenges.

Built in Unity with charming 2D pixel art, the game is currently in early beta with a playable demo on itch.io. Development has focused heavily on mechanical polish through extensive playtesting, ensuring tight, responsive controls that make platforming feel satisfying and precise.

Development Journey & Iteration

Through four major iterations over the past year, The Last Acorn evolved from a class prototype to a polished puzzle platformer. Each demo phase brought new insights, refined mechanics, and improved player experience through extensive playtesting and feedback.

Demo 1

Initial Prototype - Core Mechanics Foundation

Key Features:

  • ▸ Basic movement mechanics (jump, climb, glide)
  • ▸ Simple forest environment
  • ▸ Core player controls established
  • ▸ Initial acorn collection system

Learnings:

  • ▸ Movement felt too floaty - needed tighter controls
  • ▸ Visual feedback for interactions was unclear
  • ▸ Players wanted more environmental variety
Demo 2

Refined Movement & Cave Introduction

Key Improvements:

  • ▸ Tightened player physics and air control
  • ▸ Added cave sections with hazards
  • ▸ Introduced spike mechanics for precision challenges
  • ▸ Enhanced visual feedback for player actions

Learnings:

  • ▸ Cave difficulty spike too high for new players
  • ▸ Gliding mechanics needed more polish
  • ▸ Players enjoyed the risk/reward of dangerous sections
Demo 3

Polished Mechanics & Interactive Elements

Key Improvements:

  • ▸ Added interactive NPCs (snakes with contact damage)
  • ▸ Balanced difficulty curve across sections
  • ▸ Polished gliding with better air control
  • ▸ Improved level design flow and pacing

Learnings:

  • ▸ Players loved the simple, responsive controls
  • ▸ Cave sections needed clearer visual cues
  • ▸ More enemy variety would add depth
Demo 4

Current Build - Playtested & Refined

Final Refinements:

  • ▸ Fully polished movement system through extensive playtesting
  • ▸ Enhanced pixel art and environmental details
  • ▸ Optimized difficulty progression
  • ▸ Refined hazard placement and visual clarity

Current Status:

  • ▸ Public beta available on itch.io
  • ▸ Mechanics feel tight and responsive
  • ▸ Ready for content expansion and Steam development
  • ▸ Strong positive feedback from playtesters

Key Takeaways from Development

Simplicity Wins

Through playtesting, we learned that simple, polished mechanics trump complex systems. Players value responsiveness over feature bloat.

Playtesting is Essential

Every iteration was driven by player feedback. Watching real users interact with the game revealed issues we never would have found internally.

Iterate Fearlessly

We weren't afraid to cut features or completely rework systems. Demo 2's cave difficulty was too high, so we rebuilt the entire progression.

Polish Takes Time

The movement system went through dozens of small tweaks. The final 10% of polish took 50% of the development time, but it's what makes the game feel great.

Key Features & Mechanics

Fluid Movement System

Custom-built player movement featuring three core mechanics: climbing on trees, precise jumping, and smooth gliding. All three mechanics work seamlessly together and are equally viable options for traversal, creating diverse gameplay possibilities.

Dual Environment Design

Navigate through peaceful forest sections with tree climbing and exploration, then test your skills in dangerous cave sections featuring spikes on floors and ceilings. Cave segments emphasize precise gliding through the air with minimal room for error.

Interactive Hazards

Encounter unique NPCs and environmental challenges including snakes that damage the player on contact, deadly spikes requiring precision platforming, and various obstacles that test your mastery of the movement system.

Polished Controls

Extensive playtesting and iteration resulted in tight, responsive controls that feel satisfying to use. Every movement is deliberate and predictable, making challenging sections feel fair and rewarding to complete.

Acorn Collection

Play as a mother squirrel gathering acorns throughout the world. The collection mechanic provides goals and rewards exploration while driving players to master the movement system and navigate increasingly challenging areas.

Charming Pixel Art

Beautiful 2D pixel art style brings the world to life with detailed environments, expressive character animations, and a cohesive visual aesthetic that enhances the platforming experience.

Development Journey

CS 247G: Designed for Play

Stanford University • Academic Year Project

The Last Acorn was developed as a group project for Stanford's CS 247G course, which focuses on game design principles and player experience. The project allowed the team to explore core gameplay mechanics, level design, and player feedback through an iterative development process.

Current State & Beta Testing

Early Beta • Active Development

The game is currently in early beta with a playable demo available on itch.io. Development has been focused on mechanical polish, with extensive playtesting sessions to refine the movement system and ensure challenging-but-fair level design. Player feedback has been instrumental in improving the game feel and responsiveness of the controls.

Technical Achievements

Unity Engine • Custom Systems

Built from the ground up in Unity, the project features custom-built player movement systems that seamlessly integrate climbing, jumping, and gliding mechanics. The level design process involved careful iteration to ensure each mechanic feels essential and provides unique solutions to platforming challenges. The team balanced accessibility with difficulty through thoughtful placement of hazards and environmental storytelling.

Future Plans

Steam Release • Expanded Content

Plans for the future include releasing the game on Steam with expanded content, additional levels, and seasonal elements that add variety to the gameplay experience. The team aims to build upon the solid mechanical foundation with more diverse environments, challenges, and narrative elements that deepen the player's connection to the mother squirrel's journey.
